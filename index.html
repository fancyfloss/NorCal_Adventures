🌫️ A Note on the Fog & The Vibe

San Francisco operates on microclimates. It can be sunny in the Mission and heavily fogged in the Sunset at the exact same moment. Bring layers. Rent a bike and hit the Great Highway (which is closed to cars on weekends) to easily log a 10-mile ride with completely uninterrupted ocean views. The city rewards the curious and the perpetually hungry. [cite: 86]

🦭 Wildlife — April Is Prime Season
Sonoma Coast / Point Reyes
Gray Whale Migration
Peak Season (April)
Up to 26,000 gray whales pass the California coast northbound in April heading from Baja Mexico to Alaska. [cite: 524] Best land viewing: Bodega Head (Sonoma), Chimney Rock (Point Reyes), Marin Headlands. [cite: 525] Bring binoculars. [cite: 526]
Año Nuevo State Park
Elephant Seals
Self-Guided in April
Walk among hundreds of Northern elephant seals on the beach. [cite: 522] Males can weigh 2,000 lbs and look genuinely prehistoric up close. January-born pups are weaning and learning to swim. [cite: 523]
Moss Landing
Sea Otters at Elkhorn Slough
Kayak Tour
The highest concentration of sea otters in California. [cite: 517] In spring, mothers float on their backs with pups on their chests in the kelp. [cite: 518] Kayak tours put you eye-level. [cite: 519]
